# Instructions for Codex

This repository is being used mainly for academic writing and research-project development, not only for programming.

## User writing standard

When revising research proposals, assignments, or academic documents, follow these standards:

- Keep a natural graduate-student academic tone.
- The writing should be readable, logical, and complete, but not overly polished or template-like.
- Avoid a repeated mechanical structure such as: concept -> example -> method -> conclusion in every paragraph.
- Do not intentionally add grammar mistakes, but allow natural sentence-length variation and less uniform transitions.
- Keep the content closely aligned with the question, prompt, research topic, and methodology.
- Prefer clear explanation over decorative academic wording.
- Reduce the feeling of AI-generated text by making the flow more like a student explaining their own research logic.
- Keep the research logic stable: C26 manufacturing, backward GVC participation, REER/export elasticity, OECD TiVA, BIS REER, and China/Malaysia as focal interpretation cases.
- Do not invent unsupported literature, data results, or empirical findings.
- If expanding the proposal, expand by strengthening the argument, literature connection, data/method explanation, and research boundaries, rather than adding empty filler.

## Research topic

The main research proposal studies whether the dampening effect of backward GVC participation on export exchange-rate elasticity is stronger in C26 manufacturing than in broader manufacturing, with China and Malaysia used as focal cases for interpretation.

Core materials are stored in `docs/research_proposal_codex_ready.md`.

## Preferred output behavior

When asked to revise the RP:

1. Read `docs/research_proposal_codex_ready.md` first.
2. Create a new file instead of overwriting the original unless explicitly asked.
3. Keep citations and references internally consistent.
4. Preserve the research questions, objectives, hypotheses, and empirical model unless the task specifically asks for changes.
5. If generating a longer version, make the expansion meaningful and section-based.
